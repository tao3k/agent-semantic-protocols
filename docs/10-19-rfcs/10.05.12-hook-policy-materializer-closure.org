#+title: Hook Policy Materializer Closure
#+startup: overview

* Status

Accepted for implementation and real-project validation.

* Problem

The client hook historically ran declarative rules first and then ran hidden
policy classifiers.  That created two policy engines, made rule ownership
unclear, and allowed installed binaries to accept a different client-config
contract from the workspace build.

* Contract

All tool deny/allow policy MUST be activated by an enabled client hook rule.
A rule MAY name one typed =decisionMaterializer=.  The materializer derives
facts and constructs the decision; it does not decide whether it participates
in the policy pipeline.  A materializer rule MUST NOT declare static routes.
Successful materialization MUST attach the activating =configRuleId=.

The accepted materializers are:

- =agent-search-json=
- =prompt-search-strategy=
- =invalid-asp-facade=
- =apply-patch=
- =source-access=

=prompt-search-strategy= may consult prompt/session observations for repeated
prime, repeated pipe, direct-read ordering, and command budget.  Those
observations remain control-plane facts; the config rule only owns activation
and precedence.  Session lifecycle, bootstrap, Org recovery, and non-tool
events remain outside the tool-policy matcher.

* Active binary freshness

Freshness MUST be based on a stable build-contract fingerprint, not equality of
whole debug/release executable hashes.  The fingerprint MUST cover the hook
client schema, embedded default client config, hook protocol/schema versions,
and the config/compiler crate versions.  =asp hook doctor= MUST report the
workspace/active fingerprint comparison and fail its contract probe when the
active parser cannot load the embedded current config.

* Acceptance

- no tool policy classifier runs after =ClientHookConfig::classify=
- invalid facade and prompt search strategy decisions carry =configRuleId=
- unknown materializers and materializer plus static routes are rejected
- workspace build and active binary expose comparable contract fingerprints
- hook doctor exercises the effective parser with the current embedded config
- config, hook, sandtable, and real-project search scenarios remain green
