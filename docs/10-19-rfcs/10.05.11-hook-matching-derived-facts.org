#+title: Hook Matching Derived Facts And Decision Materializers
#+startup: overview

* Status

Accepted for incremental implementation.

* Problem

The hook configuration engine owns static tool, command, argv, and path
matching, but several source-access policies still fall through to procedural
Rust classifiers.  Those classifiers mix three different responsibilities:

- extracting typed facts from a tool action;
- selecting allow or deny policy;
- materializing a provider-aware route.

This makes =configRuleId= disappear from decisions and leaves a second policy
surface outside the hook matching engine.

* Contract

One hook rule may name an optional =decisionMaterializer=.  Static matching is
still performed by the rule engine.  After a rule matches, the materializer may
use the current runtime, tool action, command tokens, and derived paths to
produce a provider-aware decision.

The materializer is not an independent classifier:

- it is reachable only from a matched, enabled rule;
- its decision inherits the matched rule's =configRuleId=;
- returning no decision continues evaluation with the next rule;
- it may extract dynamic facts and routes, but may not introduce an unnamed
  policy branch.

The first materializer is =agent-search-json=.  It preserves provider policy
and owner-versus-lexical route selection while moving the policy entry point
into a declarative built-in rule.

* Migration order

1. Add =decisionMaterializer= to the shared hook client-config schema.
2. Compile and validate the materializer as a typed enum.
3. Add the built-in =deny-agent-search-json= rule.
4. Remove the procedural search-json fallback from command classification.
5. Add derived facts for direct reads, source-directory enumeration, bulk
   dumps, and structured patch paths.
6. Migrate their policy decisions into named built-in rules and retain only
   typed fact extraction and route materialization in Rust.

* Invariants

- Existing project rules may override a built-in rule by stable rule id.
- Provider and language behavior remains data-driven; no provider list is
  hard-coded into the hook runtime.
- Every migrated deny decision contains =configRuleId=.
- Lifecycle, receipt, and ASP facade validation remain outside command policy
  matching.
