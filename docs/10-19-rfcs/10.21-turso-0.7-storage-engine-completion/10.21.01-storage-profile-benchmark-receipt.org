* Scenario assertions

- The real-Busy benchmark holds a second =BEGIN IMMEDIATE= writer, releases it
  after 2 ms, and fails the benchmark if =busyCount= is zero.
- The MVCC append benchmark consumes the typed =retryCount=, =busyCount=,
  =snapshotConflictCount=, and =retryDelayMs= receipt fields.
- Both read benchmarks execute database-side keyset queries.  The MVCC unit
  scenario separately proves =limit + 1= and =(createdAtMs,eventId)= tie-break
  behavior.
- The maintenance benchmark appends a batch, calls Turso 0.7 =cacheflush= on
  every long-lived lane, and consumes the persisted file-size receipt.

* Conservative interpretation

This receipt is a fixed baseline, not a claim that one profile is globally
fastest.  The current single-stream workload favors Compatibility Immediate.
MVCC must be judged on independent-writer concurrency, conflict rate, recovery,
checkpoint growth, resident memory, and long-ingestion stability.

The MVCC append distribution is noisy and Criterion reported that the configured
5 s sampling target was insufficient.  A closure receipt therefore still needs
explicit p50/p95/p99, RSS, database/WAL bytes over time, and a longer ingestion
window.  Turso 0.7 exposes =cacheflush= but not a public passive-checkpoint
completion counter; the maintenance receipt records
=checkpointCounterObservable=false= instead of inventing a value.
